# Check your book

SEED.html checks your book for accessibility problems in context — right where you're writing — so you can keep it accessible as you go, rather than fixing things at the end.

## Accessibility check

While you edit, click _Accessibility_{.ui} in the preview header to check the current chapter. SEED.html scans the page and lists what it finds — things like an image missing its description, or text too faint against its background — with a count beside the button. When the chapter is clean, it says **No accessibility issues found**.

Because it runs on the chapter you're working on, you can see how your content and style choices affect your book's accessibility, and adjust as you go.

When something is flagged, fix it where it comes from: a missing image description is added in your chapter text, where the image is placed, and text too faint against its background is a colour choice, fixed in the book's styles. Check again as you work — the count falls as the chapter comes clean.

> **Try it.** Click _Accessibility_{.ui} on the chapter you're writing now and see what it finds.
